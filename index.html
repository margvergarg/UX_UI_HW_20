TrekMates

The Problem

Travelers are frustrated with being unable to find friends to go on trips with. Whether their motivation is fear or fun, they feel reluctant to travel alone

The Solution

TrekMates offers a safe, reliable way to match with similar people to travel with.

My Role

UX Researcher, UI Designer (individual project)

Tools

Figma, Miro, Adobe XD, Procreate, pencil and paper

Timeline

Four Weeks

User Research

We started with creating a proto persona to imagine what our ideal user might be like.

User Interview Plan

Background

In my experience, I find it difficult to travel alone, especially when it comes to places I’ve never been to before. Something that has held me back from traveling more is that my friends don’t have the same availability or budget to travel that I do. On Tinder and Hinge, there are plenty of profiles that express interest in finding someone to travel with, so I wondered how an app like that might work, while still centering safety.

Goals

As a user research, I wanted to understand traveler’s thought processes about meeting new people while traveling and about traveling alone.

Methodology

We interviewed participants individually over a zoom call, each one lasting approximately 10-15 minutes. We alternated who asked questions and who took notes.

Research Questions

Are people willing to meet new people while traveling?

What apps or social media do people currently use to meet new people while traveling?

What are people’s worries or concerns about traveling alone?

What worries or concerns do people have about meeting new people while traveling?

User Interview Notes

User Insight

After we finished the interviews, we collected and compiled our notes in Miro with each color representing a different participant.

Affinity Diagram

We then organized the sticky notes into categories and were able to pinpoint several trends among our participants.

Empathy Map

From our Affinity Diagram, we were able to synthesize the data and further improve upon our proto persona.

User Persona

Greg Anderson 2.0

We took the data from our interviews and created a user persona who more accurately described our users.

Definition and Ideation

User Insight

Greg, a barista/photographer who frequently gets restless, needs to find reliable people he can travel with, who share his interests and availability, because he feels stressed and anxious at the thought of traveling alone; he also believes that trips are more worthwhile when there’s someone to share the experiences with.

Problem Statement

TrekMates was designed to safely pair individuals with similar interests and availability for group travel. We have observed that even though users had several things in common and got to know each other prior to the trip, they still felt awkward traveling together, which is causing them not to use our app for future trips. How might our application better match people so they have a better experience on their trips together?

Ideation

Feature Prioritization Matrix

Using the "I like, I wish, What if" ideation method, we brainstormed potential features for TrekMates. We then organized those ideas into a feature prioritization matrix to gain a better understanding of which features to pursue sooner.

Value Proposition

The buddy system reimagined for travel.

Our company developed TrekMates, an app designed to safely match individuals for group trips based on compatibility and availability.

Storyboard

We wrote a UX scenario to better understand what user's might be experiencing while discovering and using the app. We then fleshed the scenario out a bit more with a storyboard and user journey map, which detailed what frustrations users might face a bit more.

Prototyping

User Flow Diagram

Before we started sketching, we created a user flow diagram to map out the interactions the users would be able to make in the onboarding process.

Paper Prototype

We started with some rough sketches using procreate, and we then used Adobe XD to create a paper prototype

Lo-Fi Digital Wireframes

We then digitized those wireframes into a low-fidelity prototype.

Guerilla Testing Plan

Objective

The objective was to find any kinks that might have been overlooked in the prototype and then quickly fix them.

Target User

Someone with experience and understanding of dating and social apps, who also loves travel and meeting new people. They are cautious enough to not want to travel alone but not so much that they would never upload a picture of their ID to an app.

Questions

Is the onboarding process too long? Does the ability to skip not required sections sidestep click fatigue? Is verifying your identity two ways too excessive?

Tasks

Verify identity

Finish creating an account

additional information to their profile

Match with a proto profile

Results

While we found a few flaws in the prototype, they were able to be quickly fixed, and in the end, all five users were able to get through the whole onboarding process and start matching with proto profiles.

Everyone who tested the prototype said it was straightforward and easy to navigate.

One user said he would never upload a picture of his ID in real life and that if he was doing it in real life, he would have skipped the not required steps.

Another user said that it was just the right length, but they were starting to feel click fatigue.

Final Product

Hi-Fi Prototype

Final Thoughts

Looking back, it probably would have been easier if I had focused less on the onboarding process and more on the features of the app. I wish I could have let users interact with the home page more besides just swiping on proto profiles. I learned how deceptively time intensive wireframing can be, and that I shouldn’t be super precious about every detail when doing lo-fi prototypes. The design process can be a bit exhaustive, creating so many iterations, but I understand why reiterating is so important. Overall I’m proud of my work so far, and I wouldn’t mind pitching it to make it into a real app one day!
